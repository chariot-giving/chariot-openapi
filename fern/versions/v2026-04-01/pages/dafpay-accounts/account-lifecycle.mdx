A DAFpay account is the link between one of your donors and DAFpay. The account moves through a small set of statuses depending on whether you or the donor took the first step.

## Statuses

| Status | Meaning | Grants can submit? |
|--------|---------|--------------------|
| `requested` | The donor signed up on DAFpay and selected your organization. You haven't linked them yet. | No |
| `pending` | The account is linked between DAFpay and your system, but the donor hasn't verified ownership via the `auth_code`. | No |
| `approved` | The donor has verified ownership. | Yes |
| `disabled` | Deactivated by you or the donor. Can be re-enabled. | No |

## Three onboarding paths

### Path 1 — You create the account first

```
You create the account ───► pending ─────────────► approved
(POST /accounts)              (auth_code +          (donor signs up via
                               invite_url           invite URL, or enters
                               returned)            auth_code manually)
```

This is the most common path. Create the account in your system, share the `invite_url` with your donor through a trusted channel, and DAFpay auto-links and approves the account when the donor signs up.

### Path 2 — The donor signs up without an invite

```
Donor signs up on DAFpay ───► requested ───► pending ───────► approved
(searches for your DAF)        (you haven't    (you set         (donor enters
                                linked them)   external_        auth_code)
                                               account_id)
```

When you see a `requested` account, acknowledge it by setting `external_account_id` via [PATCH](/api/dafpay-accounts/update). That moves the account to `pending` and waits for the donor to verify via the `auth_code`.

### Path 3 — Donor signs up via your invite URL

```
Donor signs up via invite URL ─────────────────────► approved
```

If the donor signs up using an `invite_url` you generated, verification is automatic and the account skips straight to `approved`.

## Disabling and re-enabling

Either you or the donor can `disable` an account at any time. Re-enabling an account restores it to its previous status (typically `approved`). The `disabled_by` field on the account tells you who disabled it.
