title: "Account Lifecycle"
subtitle: "How a DAFpay account moves between requested, pending, approved, and disabled."
hidden: false
---

A DAFpay account links one of your donors to DAFpay. Who creates the account first—**the donor on DAFpay** or **you via the API**—determines the starting status and how it reaches **`approved`**, the active state in which donors can submit grant requests.

## Statuses

| Status | Meaning | Grants can submit? |
|--------|---------|--------------------|
| `requested` | The donor used DAFpay first and selected your organization. They have an `auth_code` to give you; you have not activated the account yet. | No |
| `pending` | You created the account with `POST /accounts`. The donor has not yet entered the `auth_code` in DAFpay. | No |
| `approved` | The account is active and verified. | Yes |
| `disabled` | Deactivated by you or the donor. Can be re-enabled. | No |

## Two ways to reach `approved`

### 1 — Donor first (`requested` → `approved`)

The donor discovers your organization on DAFpay before you create an account. DAFpay creates an account in **`requested`** status and gives the donor an **`auth_code`**.

1. The donor shares that `auth_code` with you through a trusted channel.
2. You call [POST /v1/dafpay/accounts/activate](/api/dafpay-accounts/activate) with the `auth_code` (and optionally `external_account_id` to store your internal ID). Chariot resolves the account by code and moves it straight to **`approved`**.

The account is **created by the donor flow** (not by your `POST /v1/dafpay/accounts`).

```
Donor uses DAFpay first ──► requested ──► approved
                             (donor shares     (POST /v1/dafpay/accounts/activate
                              auth_code)        with that auth_code)
```

### 2 — DAF Provider first (`pending` → `approved`)

You create the account with [POST /v1/dafpay/accounts](/api/dafpay-accounts/create). It starts in **`pending`**. DAFpay returns an **`auth_code`** and **`invite_url`**.

1. Share the `auth_code` (and optionally the `invite_url`) with the donor through a trusted channel.
2. The donor enters the `auth_code` in DAFpay. DAFpay then moves the account to **`approved`**.

You **cannot** move `pending` → `approved` yourself via the API; only the donor completing verification inside DAFpay performs that transition.

The account is **created by you** (`POST /v1/dafpay/accounts`).

```
You POST /accounts ──► pending ──► approved
                         (you share          (donor enters
                          auth_code)          auth_code in DAFpay)
```

## Disabling and re-enabling

Either you or the donor can `disable` an account at any time. Re-enabling an account restores it to its previous status (typically `approved`). The `disabled_by` field on the account tells you who disabled it.
