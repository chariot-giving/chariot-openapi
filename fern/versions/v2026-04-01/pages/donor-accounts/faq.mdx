<AccordionGroup>

<Accordion title="Which verification flow should I use?">
You can support either or both flows — they are not mutually exclusive.

**Donor-Initiated Verification** works well for donors who give at the point of inspiration with no pre-setup. The donor submits a grant, receives a code by email, and passes it to you — one API call to verify.

**DAF-Initiated Setup** works well when you want explicit donor consent before any grant is submitted. Donors authenticate with your portal and opt in, giving you more control before any grant activity.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can one donor have multiple Donor Accounts?">
No — a donor has one Donor Account. That Donor Account can be associated with multiple Funds, where each Fund represents a specific giving pool within their DAF.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is `external_id` and when should I set it?">
`external_id` is your internal identifier for the user's account. Setting it links the Donor Account to your internal record and makes reconciliation easier. You can set it on [Create Donor Account](/api/donor-accounts/create) or update it later via [Update Donor Account](/api/donor-accounts/update).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if a donor loses their verification code?">
Codes expire after a fixed window regardless of flow. When a code expires, the donor will need a new one.

For **Donor-Initiated Verification**: The next Grant Request the donor submits will trigger a new token and email automatically.

For **DAF-Initiated Setup**: You can also [Revoke](/api/authorization-tokens/revoke) the existing token early and [Create](/api/authorization-tokens/create) a new one, then surface the new code to the donor. Never share two valid tokens at once.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I move a Grant Request to `initiated` before the Donor Account is approved?">
No. A Grant Request in `awaiting_account_approval` cannot be moved to `initiated` until its linked Donor Account is approved. Once the Donor Account is approved, the Grant Request automatically advances to `awaiting_daf_submission` and becomes eligible for you to act on.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens to open Grant Requests when a Donor Account is rejected?">
All open Grant Requests tied to a rejected Donor Account are automatically moved to `canceled`. You do not need to cancel them individually. A `grant.updated` webhook is emitted for each one.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can a decided Donor Account be changed?">
Donor Account approval decisions are terminal — once `approved` or `rejected`, the status cannot be changed. However, an approved Donor Account can be disabled, which prevents future Grant Requests from being processed without affecting past activity.
</Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>
